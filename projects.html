mSense-Cancer

Mobile Sensing for Cancer Survivor Quality of Life

Cancer survivors experience significant post-treatment symptoms including fatigue, pain, and psychological distress that often go unmonitored between clinical visits. This project develops a passive smartphone sensing system that continuously monitors physical activity, sleep quality, social engagement, and mood-related behaviors to predict patient-reported outcomes in breast and colorectal cancer survivors.

Research Contributions

  • ▸Novel multimodal feature extraction pipeline for cancer symptom prediction
  • ▸Semi-supervised learning to handle missing labels in longitudinal data
  • ▸IRB-approved study with N=80 cancer survivors at UVA Health
Mobile Sensing Semi-supervised ML Oncology

MultitaskMH

Multitask Learning for mHealth Intervention Availability

Just-in-time adaptive interventions (JITAIs) require knowing when a person is both available and receptive to a notification. This project builds a multitask learning framework that simultaneously predicts availability, receptivity, stress level, and activity type from continuous smartphone sensor streams — enabling smarter intervention timing.

Research Contributions

  • ▸Shared-private multitask architecture outperforming single-task by 23% AUC
  • ▸Uncertainty-aware prediction to avoid over-burdening users
  • ▸Validated on HeartSteps and GLOBEM benchmark datasets
Multitask Learning JITAIs mHealth

NeuroPose

Wearable IMU Sensing for Neuromuscular Disease Assessment

Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis (ALS) progresses rapidly and requires frequent objective assessment of motor function. NeuroPose uses a network of body-worn IMU sensors to extract gait, limb movement, and postural control features, feeding into a longitudinal deep learning model that tracks disease progression and predicts clinical outcomes.

Research Contributions

  • ▸Multi-site wearable IMU dataset for ALS and healthy controls (N=120)
  • ▸Temporal CNN-LSTM for longitudinal motor function tracking
  • ▸Clinical validation against ALSFRS-R scale (r=0.87)
Wearable IMU Gait Analysis ALS / Neurology

DigitalPhen

Smartphone-Based Digital Phenotyping Platform

An open-source Android/iOS data collection framework for longitudinal digital phenotyping studies. DigitalPhen collects 15+ behavioral and contextual streams (GPS, accelerometry, Bluetooth proximity, app usage, screen state, ambient audio features) with built-in privacy controls and configurable EMA prompts.

Research Contributions

  • ▸Privacy-preserving passive sensing with differential privacy guarantees
  • ▸Used across 3 IRB-approved studies (N=250 participants total)
  • ▸Open-source toolkit with preprocessing pipelines and ML baselines
